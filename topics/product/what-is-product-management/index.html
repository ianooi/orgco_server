What is product management?

Product management is key to the success of every business yet it is often misunderstood with grave consequences.

A product is the part of the business that the customer wants to pay for. It includes a set of capabilities that enhance the customer. A product team comprises of everyone who has input into the enhancement, from marketing and sales to development and delivery.

A product manager is the leader of the product. Like all great leaders, they must have a vision. Not a vision for what they want to build, but a vision for the customer. A product manager must establish an evidence-backed vision for the customer that gains the acceptance of the team.

The vision must be transformed into something actionable. A product manager must establish a time-phased strategy explaining who to target, what to build and why. By leveraging the expertise of each function, it must also include the how. The team now knows where they are headed and how they will get there.

Many product managers fall into the trap of being too focused on building features. Building is just one of the many aspects to consider. The product manager must look at the entire customer journey from pre-sale to renewal, to see how the proposed work fits into the bigger picture.

The goal for a product manager is to figure out how to create recurring customers in the target market. However, an approach that works today may not work tomorrow because the market can quickly change. It is up to the product manager to revalidate everything, adjust and lead the team accordingly.
