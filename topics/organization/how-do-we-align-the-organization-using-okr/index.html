How do we align the organization using OKR?

Misalignment is detrimental to growth and finding an effective framework for aligning actions with goals still eludes many.

OKR is a goal‑setting tool which stands for Objective and Key Result. It was designed to help companies improve alignment and transparency, and become more outcome-oriented.

The objective describes what you want to achieve. The key result describes how you will achieve the objective and helps you track your progress. There are usually 3-5 key results per objective.

The concept is straightforward but many have struggled to implement it. They either end up creating a giant to‑do list, or specifying key results that when completed do not deliver the objective.

In our opinion, OKRs should be viewed as a tool for problem decomposition. The objective is the problem that the entire team wants to solve and the key results are a list of possible solutions.

Objective

  • Raise $5m in the next round of funding.

Key Results

  • Increase revenue from $1m in FY 2018 to $3m in FY 2019.
  • Maintain expense to revenue ratio of 2:1 in FY 2019.

The key results then become the objectives for the individual functions in the team. Through cascading OKRs, the individual functions are aligned and working backwards from the desired outcome.

Our approach enables everyone in the team to appreciate the role that they and their teammates have in helping the team achieve its objective. As such, OKRs can be an effective tool for organizational alignment as long as it is implemented and used correctly.
