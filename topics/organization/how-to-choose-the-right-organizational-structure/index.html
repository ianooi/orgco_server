How to choose the right organizational structure

The wrong organizational structure inhibits growth and many are still searching for the right structure with little success.

Organizational structures are the foundation for building organizations that scale. It drives how teams communicate with each other, and how decisions are made, knowledge is shared and performance is measured.

Organizational structures, however, have tradeoffs. Your goal is to be aware of these tradeoffs and manage it as best you can. There is no one-size-fits-all solution. You should choose a structure that matches the type of work you do and the talent you have access to.

The most commonly adopted structure is hierarchical with stacked layers of management. This structure works by having people at the top issue commands for those beneath to follow. This creates a team of instruction takers and is most effective for work that is repetitive and unvarying (e.g. the production function of a manufacturing business).

The flat organization is an alternate structure that companies have been experimenting with recently. It organizes people into a network of cross-functional teams. Each team works autonomously and has the capabilities to satisfy customers end-to-end. These teams can respond to changes quickly without having to wait for decisions up top.

How does this structure work? Product determines the user capabilities that will help customers and attract buyers. Development & Ops deliver the capabilities. Marketing & Sales convert the buyers into customers. Success helps customers obtain their desired capabilities. People & Systems help the functions operate at maximum performance. Each team is designed for rapid experimentation.

When choosing an organizational structure, you should focus on the next 12-18 months. Avoid trying to find a long-term solution because conditions will change and so should your organization.
