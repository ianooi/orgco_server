Teams

We design team structures that align members through goals and metrics, and prioritize outcome over output.

Initially, even with undefined roles, a team can perform well. Everyone is aligned and does a bit of everything. Whatever it takes to build the product and acquire customers.

Then as the startup grows, the workload expands and the team is split into multiple functional teams. People then focus on their own functions and alignment suffers. Mistakes are made. Targets are missed. Morale falls.

It may be tempting to adopt the functional team structure of large, traditional organizations because it is common and easy to implement. But grouping people from the same function is creating a group, not a team.

A team must have common goals, defined customers and the capabilities to satisfy customers end-to-end. It is cross-functional by design, comprising of functional experts who have full ownership of their domain.

Such a structure yields many benefits. Team members are fully focused on their team goals and customers. They work independently but are aligned. They have boundaries and handoffs yet collaborate.

How we can help

Team structure. We design structures optimized for achieving business goals and inspiring team members. We work with individual teams to define the roles and responsibilities necessary for executing the business strategy.

Team metrics. We design metrics that are clear and practical to help teams manage their progress. Our metrics are designed to measure the team’s outputs (efficiency) and outcomes (effectiveness).
