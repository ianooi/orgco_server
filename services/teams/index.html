Teams

We design team structures that align people with common goals, incentivize them through metrics and prioritize outcome over output.

Initially, even with undefined functions, a team can perform well. Everyone is aligned and does a bit of everything. Whatever it takes to build the product and acquire customers.

Then as the team grows, the workload expands. The team is split into multiple functional teams. People then focus on their own functions and alignment becomes difficult. Mistakes are made. Targets are missed. Team morale falls.

It may be tempting to adopt the functional team structure of large, traditional organizations because it is easy and common. But grouping people from the same function is a group, not a team.

A team must have common goals, defined customers and the capabilities to satisfy its customers end-to-end. It is cross-functional by nature, comprising of experts who have full ownership of their domain.

Such a structure yields many benefits. Team members are fully focused on their team goals and customers. They work independently but are aligned. They have boundaries and handoffs, yet collaborate.

How we can help

Team structure. We work with organizations to design optimal team structures, and define the roles and responsibilities necessary to execute on the product strategy.

Success metrics. When business objectives and context are made clear and transparent to teams, it allows them to align their actions with those objectives. Our aim is to help teams define measurable success metrics that maximizes business outcome over output.
